There are 2 main types of patterns. Those represented with names, and those represented with siteswap!

Named Tricks

Named Tricks are the most common, basic and popular tricks that are done. For ease of communication, jugglers came up with names for each one!

The 'Cascade' is the most common, easiest and generic juggling pattern. It's the first juggling pattern anyone learns, and it consists of each hand throwing in an even, alternating rhythm. The cascade can only be juggled with an odd amount of props.

Siteswap Tricks

For patterns that don't have names, siteswap is used! Siteswap is the mathematical representation of a juggling pattern using numbers! Each individual digit represents a certain beat of the juggling pattern. It's confusing at first, but you'll soon get the knack of it!

633 is a common siteswap to begin with. One juggles 4 balls, throws 1 up, quickly juggles 3 underneath, then goes back into a 4 ball pattern. Keep practicing until you can do an infinite cycle!

9751 is a visually stunning but incredibly difficult trick. Each ball is thrown lower than the previous one, making the first ball you throw, the last you catch. Strength, accuracy and a strong neck is needed for this one!
