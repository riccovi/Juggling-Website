Toss Jugglers

Toss juggling is the most common form of juggling you'll see in circuses, at parks and on the streets. Toss juggling is simply the act of tossing multiple props up into the air, catching them, and throwing them up again. A continual cycle of catching and throwing to keep all props aloft in the air. Easier said than done!

Anthony Gatto

Regarded by many as the world's greatest juggler. Anthony Gatto is a name known amongst all. A juggling prodigy born in Manhattan, world-class by the age of nine and began performing in Las Vegas at 10 years old. The only juggler to have ever won the Golden Clown Award with numerous world records still unbroken 15 years later.

Delaney Bayles

Dubbed as the "most technically gifted female toss juggler of all time", Delaney keeps wowing her fans with never-before-seen tricks from backcrosses to overheads. Voted the most popular juggler by Luke Burrage in 2020 and 2021.

Bounce Jugglers

Bounce juggling is, you guessed it, juggling with bounces! Unlike toss jugglers who throw towards the air, bounce jugglers throw towards the ground. They use a specific type of material to make the ball and the surface extra bouncy. Bounce Juggling is arguably more difficult than toss juggling, as one only has so much space between your hands and the ground.

Alan Sulc

Part of the Berouesek circus family, Alan started juggling at the age of 7, and started performing at the age of 12. One of the best bounce numbers jugglers, Alan holds world records in bounce juggling 8 to 12 balls!

Eden Zak

Eden Zak is a bounce juggler from Israel. At the age of only 8, he became the first person to qualify a 10 ball bounce. (20 catches) In 2014, he set the current 11 ball bounce world record of 12 catches.

Diabolo Jugglers

Highly popular in Japan, Diabolo also commonly called Chinese yo-yo, is a unique type of juggling. Consisting of an axle and two hourglass cups, as well as a seperate string with two hand sticks, the conservation of angular momentum is used to toss, juggle and spin the diabolo. Advanced tricks include whip catches, finger grinds, star cradles, and introducing 2 or more diabolos on one string.

Hiroki Kamei

In the world of diabolo, Hiroki Kamei may be the best the world has ever seen. Inventing and performing tricks once thought impossible, Hiroki made a name for himself when he won the 2016 IJA competition becoming the first diabolist to do so. For anyone new to Diabolo, Hiroki's performance is a must-watch.

Guillaume Karpowicz

Known for his creativity and unique style, Guillaume Karpowicz from Germany is the only person to have qualified both 5 diabolos high and low. He was also the first person to 6 diabolos low in 2016. Guillaume holds world records in both 5 and 6 diabolo high, as well as 6-10 diabolo passing.

Pieter Slachmuylders

Another amazing numbers diabolist, Pieter started practicing diabolo at the age of 13 in his home country of Belgium. He has gone on to achieve the 3 diabolos high world record of 5 minutes and 53 seconds, and holds the 10 diabolo passing world record of 13 catches with Guillaume Karpowicz.
